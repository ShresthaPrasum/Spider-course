Where chess began

Chess came from an old game in India called chaturanga around the 500s or 600s CE. The game moved to Persia and was called shatranj. Later it spread to the Muslim world, then to Europe and Russia.

Change to modern rules

In Europe, around the 1400s, some pieces changed moves. The queen and bishop became stronger. This change made the game faster and led to the modern game.

Chess and ideas

Chess has been part of books, art, and science. In recent years it also helped computer researchers. Strong computer programs can check many moves very fast.

Short timeline

  • 6th century — chaturanga in India.
  • 7th–9th century — spread to Persia (shatranj).
  • 15th century — big rule changes in Europe.
  • 19th century — first modern tournaments.
  • 1997 — a famous computer match showed strong AI play.

This is a short view of the history. Many details change by place and time.
