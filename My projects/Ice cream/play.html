Basic rules

Chess is on an 8 by 8 board. Each side has 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two bishops, two knights, and eight pawns. The aim is to checkmate the opponent's king. Checkmate means the king is under attack and cannot move to safety.

How pieces move (short)

  • King: one square any way.
  • Queen: any number of squares straight or diagonal.
  • Rook: any number of squares straight.
  • Bishop: any number of squares diagonal.
  • Knight: move in an L shape (two then one). It can jump over pieces.
  • Pawn: move forward one (or two on first move); capture one square diagonal. If a pawn reaches the far row it can become a queen or another piece.

Opening tips

  1. Control the center squares with pawns or pieces.
  2. Bring out knights and bishops early.
  3. Keep the king safe. Castling helps.

Benefits of chess

  • Thinking: plan moves and choices.
  • Problem solving: find the best move under limits.
  • Focus: stay calm and watch the board.
  • Memory: learn patterns and ideas.
  • Good sport: learn to win and lose politely.

Practice ideas

Play slow games, solve small puzzles, and review your games. Try to learn one new idea each week.
